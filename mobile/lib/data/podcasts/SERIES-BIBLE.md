# Series Bible — *The Long Way Home* (Bou Mie)

**Language:** Izon (Ịzọn) · Kolokuma dialect (the education standard)
**Region:** Niger Delta, Bayelsa State, Nigeria
**Audience:** English speakers learning Izon (French gloss included throughout)
**Format:** recurring-character audio drama · 9 episodes · 3 levels × 3 styles/lengths
**Status:** authored, `isActive: false` — **awaiting native-speaker recording & heritage verification**

> **The one rule that shapes everything:** *no interface language is ever spoken in
> the audio* (per `userio-docs/BEELI_PODCAST_LESSON_TEMPLATE_v2.md`). English/French
> live only on the screen (card titles, glosses, cultural notes). The learner is
> inside Izon from the first second. Comprehension is carried by situation,
> repetition, visuals, and the story — never by translation.

---

## Logline

A city-raised young Izon, **Tari**, comes home to the creeks for the season of the
water-spirit festival and — one word at a time — learns to belong again, until they
can stand at the water and speak for the family.

## Theme

Language as belonging. You don't *study* Izon and visit some Izon things; you come
home to the Izon world and belong more deeply each episode. The season literalises
the app's mission: the diaspora learner, welcomed back by the grandmother, becomes a
keeper of the words.

## The Cast

Seven recurring characters. The **archetypes** (returnee / keeper-elder / peer /
livelihood-hand / trader / working-elder / chief) are the reusable template; another
language keeps the archetypes and renames them. Names here are all already attested in
the Beeli codebase so the podcast reuses an established world.

| Character | Archetype | Who they are | Levels |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tari** | The Returnee (learner surrogate) | ~25, raised in Yenagoa/Port Harcourt on English & Pidgin; understands scraps of Izon, shy to speak. Warm, eager, makes real beginner mistakes. In the dialogue "second pass" the **learner voices Tari**. | all |
| **Ebiere** | The Keeper | 70s, matriarch of the compound in Isampou. Unhurried, firm, dry-funny, master of proverb and story. Wants Tari to "carry the words" before she is gone. The emotional spine. | all |
| **Preye** | The Bridge | ~24, village-dwelling cousin, fully fluent, teasing, quick. Code-switch energy but always speaks Izon on-mic. The comic engine. | beg, int |
| **Timi** | The River-hand | ~28, young fisherman cousin, practical, generous, a little boastful. Teaches by doing — counting the catch, paddling, the market. | beg, int |
| **Mama Seibi** | The Trader | 50s, sharp, theatrical market woman; the town's information hub; never loses a negotiation. | beg, int |
| **Uncle Ere** | The Working Elder | 60s, spare-spoken fisherman who reads water and weather, and quietly grieves that the fish are fewer. Carries the environmental thread. | int, adv |
| **Pa Boma (Amaokowei)** | The Chief | 70s, the town's ranking elder and orator; formal register; settles disputes with proverb; pours the festival libation. | int, adv |

**Relationship map:** Ebiere is grandmother to Tari, Preye and Timi (cousins to each
other). Uncle Ere is Ebiere's late husband's brother and Timi's mentor. Mama Seibi is
a family friend. Pa Boma is Ebiere's contemporary and the community's authority. It is
one family inside one town — the smallest complete unit of Izon social life.

**Cultural grounding:** the cast maps onto the design-course Izon culture pack
(`.claude/skills/design-course/scaffold/izon.pack.ts`): Koko→Ebiere, Uncle Ere,
Trader Seibi, Amaokowei, the Newcomer→Tari.

## The Season Arc

Tari comes home to **Isampou** for **Seigbein**, the festival of remembrance for the
**Owuamapu** (water spirits). Grandmother **Ebiere**, growing old, sets the season's
quiet stakes: she wants Tari able to stand and speak a praise for the family at the
water before she dies. Woven under the homecoming is an **environmental thread** — the
creeks are strained, the catch is thin, the water spirits are said to have withdrawn —
which gathers through the season and pays off at the closing libation, where the
blessing asks for the fish to return.

- **Beginner (A1) — arriving & belonging in the everyday.** Tari lands at the jetty
  unable to greet properly; is fed and folded into the compound; survives the market.
- **Intermediate (A2–B1) — the working & ceremonial world.** Tari goes to the river
  (and learns the fish are fewer), witnesses the Ekine masquerade, and sits in as two
  families arrange Preye's marriage.
- **Advanced (B1–C2) — the heritage crown.** Tari watches an elder end a quarrel with
  a proverb, receives the Woyengi creation story by lamplight, and at last stands at
  the festival to speak — closing the circle with the exact welcome from Episode 1,
  now fully understood.

## Style & Length, by design

Each level rotates three deliberately different shapes so learners meet the language
from three angles at every stage:

- **Skit (short):** fast two/three-hander, dialogue-only, one sharp situation. Lowest
  cognitive load, highest replay value. (Eps 1, 4, 7.)
- **Immersive story (medium):** scene-driven, narration + dialogue, one place fully
  inhabited, closing with an extended listening "story" beat. (Eps 2, 5, 8.)
- **Host-narrated (long):** a native voice frames the whole episode in Izon, with
  drills and dialogue inside the narration, and a substantial cultural set-piece.
  (Eps 3, 6, 9.)

## Recurring Motifs (continuity to hold across episodes)

- **The canoe (arụ):** the arrival boat (Ep 1), the fishing canoe (Ep 4), the disputed
  canoe and its proverb (Ep 7) — one object carrying the season's argument about
  unity and belonging.
- **The water (toru/bou):** front door (Ep 1), livelihood under strain (Ep 4), the
  spirits' home (Ep 5), the site of the libation (Ep 9).
- **The welcome "A bọọ bomaaa / Nụa":** Ebiere's first line to Tari (Ep 1) returns as
  the season's final line (Ep 9) — incomprehensible then, fully understood now.
- **The fish that grow fewer:** planted (Ep 4), explained cosmologically (Ep 5), asked
  back in the blessing (Ep 9).
- **Proverbs as an escalating skill:** an elder drops one in context (Ep 4), Ebiere
  teaches one to Tari (Ep 6), a proverb ends a quarrel (Ep 7), and Tari deploys one at
  the water (Ep 9).
