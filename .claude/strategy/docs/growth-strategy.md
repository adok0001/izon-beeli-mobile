# Beeli — Growth Strategy

*Distilled from `docs/marketing-strategy.md` §3, §6, §7, §10, §13. Decision-grade
summary; the marketing doc is the comprehensive source.*

## The bet

Own the category before a funded competitor notices it exists. Beeli is the only
audio-first platform built for African languages (70+ vs Duolingo's 4), and the
diaspora's reconnection motive produces higher retention than hobby-language
learning. Win by converting that motivation into zero-CAC, community-powered
growth.

## Three priorities

1. **Own the category** — SEO, PR, and community partnerships establish Beeli as
   *the* African language platform.
2. **Convert motivation into virality** — share cards, proverbs, and achievements
   turn emotional moments into acquisition at no media spend.
3. **Unlock institutional revenue** — activate the already-built educator portal.

## Segments (market to motivation, not mechanics)

| Segment | Who | Hook | Channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| Diaspora Reconnector (primary) | 25–45, abroad, trigger-event driven | "Your family will notice" | TikTok / IG |
| Educator / Preservationist | Teachers, organizers; mission-driven | Free classrooms, trustworthy content | LinkedIn, academic |
| Cultural Curious | 18–30, Afrodiaspora | Adinkra, proverbs, Word of the Day | Cultural content |
| Heritage Student (B2B2C) | 8–22, via a teacher | Leaderboard, multiplayer | Classroom |

## Acquisition channels (priority order)

1. **Organic social** (TikTok/IG reels, Twitter for educators) — primary discovery
2. **SEO / content** — one blog post per high-priority language per quarter; the
   web root must become a public landing page (it currently redirects to `/learn`)
3. **Diaspora community partnerships** — free classrooms for hosting events; target
   10 orgs in 30 days
4. **App Store / Play (ASO)** — see marketing-strategy §11
5. **Educator / institutional outreach** — the B2B2C funnel and revenue path
6. **Paid** — deferred until organic D30 retention is known (no leaky-bucket spend)

## The flywheel

Contributor bounties pay native speakers (in XP) to add vocabulary/audio → more
community per language → richer content → more learners → more contributors. This
is both the content-cost moat and the media story; competitors pay editorial teams
per language while Beeli's content compounds for free.

## Retention: Hook → Habit → Attachment

- **Hook (first 7 days):** onboarding first-win in ~3 min; daily challenges; Word
  of the Day; resume-on-open
- **Habit (7–30):** streaks + freeze, 30-day calendar, XP progression, leaderboard
- **Attachment (30+):** level-title identity ("I am a Guardian of Igbo"), My
  Contributions, classroom membership, multiplayer
- **Re-engagement:** lapsed-3/7/14-day push + email (copy + scheduled job; deep
  links already supported)

## 90-day launch plan

| Window | Focus | Targets |
|---|---|---|
| Days 1–14 | Foundation: verify PostHog; build web landing page; ship store listings; create @beeliapp socials; pre-write 14 days of content | Acquisition infra live |
| Days 15–30 | Seeding: 10 diaspora orgs + 5 educators; first Language of the Week (Yoruba); Product Hunt; first press pitch ("70 vs 4") | First community + press |
| Days 31–60 | Content engine: 3 blog posts (Yoruba/Swahili/Amharic); Contributor Spotlight; #BeeliChallenge; 3 classrooms | Content cadence + classrooms |
| Days 61–90 | Evaluate/monetize: retention by channel; "Support Beeli" CTA to Level 5+; first institutional partnership | Double down on the winner |

## Rules

- Every experiment needs a hypothesis, a metric, a timeline, and a kill condition.
- The first 90 days prove the motion, not scale — retained learners beat raw installs.
- Optimise for **Daily Active Learners** and retention, never vanity metrics.
- Depth-gate marketing per language — only promote languages whose first lesson is strong.
- Customer-facing copy follows `beeli-marketing` brand voice.
