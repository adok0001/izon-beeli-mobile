## Positioning statement

> For diaspora communities and African language learners worldwide who want to
> reconnect with or preserve their mother tongue, **Beeli** is the audio-first
> African language platform that covers 70+ languages with cultural depth no
> competitor touches — unlike Duolingo, Memrise, and Pimsleur, which treat African
> languages as a shallow, text-first side category.

## The killer fact

**Duolingo covers 4 African languages. Beeli covers 70+.** Verifiable, emotionally
resonant, impossible to counter quickly. It belongs in every press subject line,
ad headline, and store description.

## The threat model

Beeli faces **no direct competitor** on African languages. The risk is a legacy
platform diluting the category, not a rival taking share. So: don't compete on
gamification mechanics — compete on **authenticity and breadth**.

## Competitor comparison

| Feature | Beeli | Duolingo | Memrise | Pimsleur | Rosetta Stone | Ling / uTalk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| African languages | **70+** | 4 | ~8 | ~3 | ~2 | ~5–10 |
| Audio-first design | Yes | No | No | Yes | Partial | Partial |
| Cultural depth | Yes (Adinkra, Ge'ez, proverbs) | No | No | No | No | No |
| Community contributions | Yes (bounties) | No | No | No | No | No |
| Educator tools | Yes | Schools product | No | No | No | No |
| Free tier | All features | Limited | Limited | No | No | Freemium |
| Multiplayer | Yes | No | No | No | No | No |
| Price | Free | Free / $6.99/mo | Free / $8.99/mo | $20.95/mo | $11.99/mo | $5.99+/lang |

## The three differentiators to lead with

1. **Breadth without sacrificing depth** — Niger Delta (Izon, Isoko, Urhobo,
   Itsekiri, Nembe), endangered (Ogoni/Khana, Tamazight, Coptic, Margi), and East
   African scripts (Ge'ez) alongside Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, Swahili.
2. **Community contribution as moat** — no other app compensates native speakers
   (in XP) to build the database in real time. Product differentiator *and* media story.
3. **Endangered-language preservation** — a mission that earns editorial coverage
   no paid campaign can buy.

## Brand frame

- **Voice:** warm but not patronizing · culturally grounded · precise about
  language (name it) · aspirational without hustle-culture.
- **Taglines:** "Your language, your roots." (diaspora) · "Learn African
  languages, interactively." (educators).
- **Never:** homogenize Africa, use "exotic," safari/wildlife imagery, or remedial framing.

## Strategic implication

Duolingo's Yoruba course was made by a small team. Beeli's Izon content is built
by the Izon community itself. That distinction *is* the marketing story.
