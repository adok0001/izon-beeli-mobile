# Beeli — Business Model & Revenue Strategy

Source: `docs/marketing-strategy.md` §8 (Monetization Strategy).

---

## Who We Are

**Beeli** — an audio-first African language learning platform on iOS, Android,
and Web. 70+ languages across West, East, North, and Southern Africa. Currently
free with no in-app purchases. Owner: Tamara Adokeme.

Beeli is not a hobby-language app. Its users are reconnecting with culture,
family, and self — a deeper, more durable motivation than curiosity, and one
that produces higher retention than typical language apps.

---

## The Guiding Principle (read this before any monetization work)

**Beeli's moat is community scale. Monetization must never gate features that
drive community growth.** Language content, cultural features, contributions,
bounties, gamification, and social features stay **permanently free**. The
product grows by adding contributors and learners — not by paywalling what
already exists. Every monetization decision is judged against this: *does it ride
on top of the free product, or does it tax the flywheel?* If it taxes the
flywheel, it's wrong.

---

## Revenue Tiers (in priority order)

### Tier 1 — Free (Core, permanent)

Everything that creates community value. Non-negotiably free:

- All language learning content — lessons, audio, transcripts
- All gamification — XP, streaks, leaderboards, daily challenges, multiplayer
- All community features — feed, contributions, bounties
- All cultural content — Adinkra symbols, Ge'ez script, proverbs, dictionary
- Word review (spaced repetition)

This tier is the product. It is also the marketing engine and the moat. Treat it
as sacred.

### Tier 2 — Beeli Plus (individual premium)

**Proposed price:** $4.99/month or $39.99/year.

Adds convenience and personalization *on top of* — never carving out of — the
free experience:

- Auto-restocking streak freezes (free tier earns freezes via XP; Plus auto-stocks monthly)
- Offline audio downloads for lessons
- Advanced word-review scheduling (custom spaced-repetition intervals)
- Profile customization (badge colours, accent themes)
- Ad-free, if ads are ever introduced to free
- Early access to new language content before public release

**Hard rule:** Do **not** launch Beeli Plus until D60 retention exceeds 15%.
Premature monetization before habit formation kills free-to-premium conversion in
language apps. The 90-day plan soft-launches a "Support Beeli" CTA to high-level
users (Level 5 "Scholar"+) first — the most invested cohort — before any paywall.

### Tier 3 — Beeli Educator (institutional)

The fastest path to real revenue, because **the product is already built** — the
classroom, group management, invite-code enrollment, assignment creation,
progress tracking, and the educator content panel all exist in the codebase. This
tier is a positioning + landing-page + billing activation, not new product work.

| Plan | Monthly | Students |
|---|---|---|
| Classroom Starter | $99/month | Up to 30 |
| Classroom Pro | $199/month | Up to 100 |
| Institution | Custom | Unlimited |

**Go-to-market:** heritage language schools first (lower decision complexity,
higher motivation), then university departments (longer procurement cycles). This
is the B2B2C funnel — the teacher assigns Beeli, the students arrive.

### Tier 4 — Sponsored Language Grants

Partner with African governments, diaspora foundations, and cultural
organizations to sponsor endangered or underdeveloped language content. The
sponsor funds a bounty pool; Beeli runs the community contribution workflow and
provides a progress dashboard.

This is the **strongest-aligned** revenue line: it generates mission revenue,
earns press, *and* produces content that strengthens the free product. When in
doubt about which revenue bet to push narratively, push this one.

## Unit-Economics Notes (internal)

- **CAC target: ~zero** for the primary channels. The contributor flywheel,
  achievement share cards, proverbs, and diaspora-community partnerships are
  designed to acquire users at no media spend. Paid acquisition is deferred until
  organic D30 retention is known — paying for installs before that is filling a
  leaky bucket.
- **The contribution economy is a content-cost lever, not just engagement.**
  Every bounty fulfilled is vocabulary/audio Beeli did not have to produce
  in-house. This is why community scale is the asset: content cost falls as the
  community grows, while competitors pay editorial teams per language.
- **Educator tier is near-100% margin** — the product exists; marginal cost per
  classroom is support time only.

## Why This Wins

No competitor compensates native speakers (in XP) to build the language database
in real time, and none treats African languages as the main event rather than a
side category. The combination — free community core + contributor flywheel +
breadth no one else touches — is both the product differentiator and the media
story. Monetization is the cherry; the moat is the free flywheel. Protect it.
