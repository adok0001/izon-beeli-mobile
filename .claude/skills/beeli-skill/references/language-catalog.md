# Beeli — Language Catalog & Content Strategy

This is Beeli's equivalent of a "menu" — what we teach, how it's organized, and
which languages to prioritize for content and marketing. Source:
`docs/marketing-strategy.md` §2, §4, Appendix C, and the language data in `data/`.

---

## The Headline Fact

**70+ African languages across 9 regions.** Duolingo — the category leader with
500M+ users — covers 4. This breadth is the single most powerful asset Beeli has.
It is verifiable, emotionally resonant, and impossible for any competitor to
match quickly. Lead with it.

## Regional Groupings (how the picker is organized)

The language picker groups by region — the groupings are themselves a brand
asset, breadth made visible:

- **Niger Delta** — Izon, Isoko, Urhobo, Itsekiri, Nembe, Ogoni/Khana
- **Southwest Nigeria** — Yoruba
- **Southeast Nigeria** — Igbo
- **North Central / North Nigeria** — Hausa, Margi
- **West Africa** — Twi (Akan), Wolof, Bambara
- **East Africa** — Swahili, Amharic, Oromo, Tigrinya, Kinyarwanda, Luganda, Somali
- **North Africa** — Arabic, Tamazight (Berber), Coptic
- **Southern Africa** — Zulu, Xhosa

(Plus many more — 70+ total. Niger Delta depth and endangered-language coverage
are uniquely Beeli's.)

## The Three Content Categories That Define Beeli

1. **Major diaspora languages** — Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, Swahili, Amharic, Twi.
   Highest search volume and diaspora size; the acquisition workhorses.
2. **Niger Delta languages** — Izon, Isoko, Urhobo, Itsekiri, Nembe. No
   competitor touches these. Beeli's founder-rooted depth (the repo slug is
   `izon-beeli-mobile`) and a core authenticity story.
3. **Endangered / preservation languages** — Ogoni/Khana, Tamazight, Coptic,
   Margi. Not merely features — a preservation *mission* that earns editorial
   coverage in culture and science journalism.

## The One Content Rule

**Market to the languages where diaspora is largest AND content depth is already
sufficient to deliver a good first experience.** Never market a language whose
content would disappoint a first-time learner. A great first lesson in Yoruba
acquires and retains; a thin first lesson in a low-depth language burns an
emotionally-motivated download you can't get back. Depth-gate the marketing, not
the catalog — every language stays *available*; only some get *promoted*.

## Cultural Content (the depth no competitor touches)

Beyond lessons, Beeli ships cultural content that doubles as marketing fuel:

- **Adinkra symbols** — Akan visual proverbs; the strongest owned visual motif
- **Ge'ez / Fidel script** — the 2,000-year-old script module
- **Proverbs** — used in lessons and as free, high-performing social copy
- **Word of the Day** — daily push + daily social post, automatable from `data/`
- **Community dictionary** — searchable, contributor-built, the flywheel's output

This cultural layer is why diaspora and "cultural curious" users enter even
without taking a structured lesson — and why they stay.
